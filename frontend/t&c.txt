Terms and Conditions – Guidelines for Safety at Works
The contractor shall be responsible for the safety of all employees and labourers employed by him on works, directly or through petty contractor or subcontractor.
1. Temporary Works
All temporary works necessary for the proper execution of the works shall be provided and maintained by the contractor at his own cost.
Temporary works shall be removed by the contractor at his own expense when they are no longer required.
2. Compliance with Rules for Employee of Labour
The contractor shall comply with all applicable Central, State, Municipal, and Local laws, bylaws, rules, and regulations relating to the employment of labour.
No person below the age of 18 years shall be employed for execution of the work.
The contractor shall arrange insurance for personnel engaged in the work.
Provision of safety norms including statutory liabilities such as PF, ESI, Workmen Compensation Act, or any other relevant statute shall be ensured.
3. Observance of Environmental Regulations and Protection
The contractor shall ensure that all workmen and subcontractors comply with environmental laws and regulations.
All permit conditions, permissions, consents, and approvals issued by authorities regarding the work shall be followed.
Safety of Work and Public & Labour
The contractor shall provide and maintain at his own cost all lights, warning signage, fencing, and protective equipment necessary for safety and convenience of workers and the public.
General Safety Requirements
Safety belts and rope guards shall be used to prevent falls from height.
Workers performing welding, chipping, drilling, grinding, or cutting shall use eye protection devices.
Workers involved in wet concrete or similar work shall use waterproof boots and gloves.
Workers in wet conditions shall be provided waterproof coats and hats.
All workers shall wear approved helmets.
Tools and equipment shall be suitable for the job and maintained in safe condition.
Welding sets shall be properly earthed using insulated cable to the nearest earth pit.
Welders shall use welding shields.
Jointed, cut, or frayed wiring shall not be used.
Adequate precautions shall be taken to prevent workers from contact with electrical equipment or live circuits.
Welding, burning, and grinding operations that may cause fire shall be reported prior to commencement of work.
Contractors shall be checked for alcohol consumption before permitting work.
Wooden ladders and wooden scaffolds shall not be used inside premises.
Barricading shall be ensured for hot work, height work, excavation work, electrical work, and confined space work.
Reporting of Labour Accident
The contractor shall report every accident occurring on works to the Project Manager or Company Representatives.
Immediate arrangements shall be made for medical attention and assistance.
Compensation for affected workers or their relatives shall be paid by the contractor or insurance company in accordance with the Workmen’s Compensation Act.
The contractor shall indemnify the company against all claims, damages, penalties, and payments arising from violations by the contractor, subcontractors, or agents.
Safety Instructions
4. Hot Work
Supervisor shall conduct site rounds.
Experienced personnel with suitable PPE including apron, welding goggles/shield, and gloves shall perform the work.
Precautions shall be taken to prevent fire and sparks.
Fire extinguishers shall be readily available (CO₂ / ABC type).
Adequate ventilation shall be ensured before hot work in confined spaces.
Hoses, regulators, and flashback arrestors shall be inspected before use.
Double earthing shall be ensured for electrical welding.
LPG shall not be used for gas cutting.
Flammable and combustible materials shall be removed or covered properly.
5. Height Work (1.8 m and Above)
Open sides and openings shall be covered or barricaded.
Safety nets shall be installed where barricades cannot be used.
Safety belts and harnesses shall be secured to proper anchorage points.
Proper ladders or hoists shall be used.
Ladders shall rise at least 3 feet above landing point with correct angle of 75°.
Workers shall be medically fit for work at height.
Scaffolding shall be erected by competent persons and adequately braced.
Safe access to scaffolding shall be provided.
6. Excavation / Civil Work
Negative slopes and overhangs shall be eliminated.
Shoring materials shall be available.
Electrical cables, data cables, or pipelines shall not pass through the work area.
Workers shall wear gumboots, helmets, safety belts, goggles, and gloves as required.
Defective electrical tools shall not be used.
Caution boards and fencing shall be provided.
Toolbox training shall be conducted.
Adequate supervision by qualified personnel shall be ensured.
7. Electrical Work
Only competent electricians shall perform electrical jobs.
All live electrical parts shall be protected.
Portable electrical tools shall use 3-core cable and 3-pin plugs.
Electrical joints and connections shall be properly insulated.
Electrical equipment shall not be overloaded.
Terminal boxes shall be secured and cable extensions properly fixed.
Portable equipment shall conform to IS standards.
Tested and IS-compatible rubber gloves and safety shoes shall be used.
Ladders used for electrical work shall have electrical safety mats beneath them.
Electrically insulated and rated tools shall be used.
Lockout-Tagout (LOTO) procedures shall be followed and keys retained only with the worker.
8. Confined Space Entry
Roles such as entrant, attendant, and supervisor shall be clearly defined.
Adequate oxygen levels shall be ensured.
Proper ventilation shall be maintained.
Flammable gases shall be removed before work begins.
Emergency ladders shall be provided.
Safety belts, shoes, and helmets shall be used.
Full body aprons shall be used where required.
Trained watchmen shall be available for rescue.
Low-voltage (24V) hand lamps shall be used.
Hot work inside confined spaces shall not be performed without authorization from Safety/Security personnel.
Appropriate rescue devices shall be available.
9. Emergency Machinery Breakdown
Security or Safety In-charge shall be informed about the nature of the job.
Work shall start only after implementing all safety precautions.
Site supervision is compulsory.
